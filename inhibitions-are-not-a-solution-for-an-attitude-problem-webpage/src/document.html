Inhibitions are not a solution to an attitude problem

What made you write this?

I came into work this morning and fairly soon after arriving I was nasty to my friend Susan. Moments after the interaction I thought to myself, "What the fuck was that?". It was fairly harmless thankfully, but still bad. The day's clearest abnormality was that I had not slept the night before. I had also learnt from my friend Peter Brown the night before at XTC that poor sleep seriously affects mental performance. I learnt that yesterday. I already knew that the only reason I'm not an arrogant prick all the time is because I can stop myself. This combined knowledge helped me to see that those who are dependent on fast brain performance for their desired personality will be unable to socially interact in the way that want when their thinking is badly retarded, for example when they are sleep deprived. This is an unacceptable outcome and me and my brain will be having a chat about it's SLAs later.

Studies about reaction times should matter to you if you use inhibitions to manage underlying personality defects

It was today that I realised that the speed of your brain is actually really important. It's not about how fast you can press a button in in a lab for another dumb reaction times study, it's about if your brain will have time to stop you doing or saying something stupid. I've only read a few chapters of "Thinking, fast and slow", but I imagine that if I had perservered and read a bit more I would have learned this already.

A better idea: Be a good person so you don't need inhibitions

If you didn't lean on your brain's postprocessing power then even if you were sleep deprived, you could go into work and hang out with your friends without being a twat! What a world that would be! Using inhibitions as personality crutches works fine most of the time, but here's a news flash! "Most of the time" is not good enough.

Attitude problems and the fixed vs growth mindset

Maybe I'm just a bad person and that's just the way things are. This is the fixed mindset. I present it as bad in this context, because it doesn't help, it just makes you look like a whiny prick who feels sorry for themselves, literally about being a bad person. Pathetic! To the end of not giving this impression I try and encourage others to try to remain positive - one day I'll be a good person and if you aren't already, then you will be able to do it too :)

Irony

I wrote all this bullshit and I still have an attitude problem today and I probably still will tomorrow. I disect it to death and it continues to seem as though I am a slave to my ego. Further irony is that I proposed the topic for last night's XTC - "Deconstructing macho bullshit in software."

Did you seriously just do all that mental gymnastics just to absolve yourself of being a dickhead?
